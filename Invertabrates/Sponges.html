Sponges

Sponges are simple aquatic animals that live attached to surfaces in water.

Examples include tube sponges and barrel sponges.

They filter food particles through tiny pores in their bodies.

Sponges are among the oldest animals on Earth.

Most sponges live in oceans, but some live in freshwater.

They do not have brains, hearts, or lungs.

Sponges stay attached to rocks and underwater surfaces.

Water flows through a sponge’s body to bring food and oxygen.

Some sponges are brightly colored like yellow, red, or orange.
