Mollusks are soft-bodied animals, and many of them have shells for protection.

Examples include snails, octopuses, and clams.

They live mostly in water, but some live on land.

Octopuses are very intelligent mollusks with eight arms.

Some mollusks use shells to hide from predators.

Clams and oysters can live buried under sand.

Many mollusks move slowly using a muscular foot.

Squids are fast-swimming mollusks found in oceans.

Mollusks are important sources of food for many animals and humans.
