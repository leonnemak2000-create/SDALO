Arthropods

Arthropods are animals with jointed legs, a hard exoskeleton, and segmented bodies.

Examples include insects, spiders, and crabs.

They are the largest group of animals on Earth.

Arthropods molt their exoskeletons as they grow bigger.

Many arthropods have antennae to sense their surroundings.

Insects are the most common type of arthropod.

Crabs and lobsters are arthropods that live in water.

Spiders use silk to make webs for catching prey.

Arthropods can be found in nearly every habitat on Earth.
