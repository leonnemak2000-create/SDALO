Mammal

Mammals are warm-blooded animals with hair or fur.

They give birth to live young and feed milk to their babies.

Examples include humans, lions, elephants, and dolphins.

The Blue Whale is the largest mammal in the world.

The Platypus is one of the few mammals that lay eggs.

Bat are the only mammals that can truly fly.

The Cheetah is the fastest land mammal.

Dolphin sleep with one eye open sometimes.

Elephant can recognize themselves in mirrors.
