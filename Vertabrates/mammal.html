Mammal

Mammals are warm-blooded animals with hair or fur.

The Blue Whale is the largest mammal in the world.

Mammals give birth to live young and nurse them with milk.

Most mammals breathe air using lungs.

Examples of mammals include lions, elephants, dolphins, and humans.

Some mammals live on land while others live in water.

Bats are the only mammals capable of true flight.

Mammals have strong senses such as hearing, smell, and sight.

Polar bears have thick fur to survive in cold environments.

Many mammals care for their babies until they can survive on their own.
