Amphibians

Amphibians are cold-blooded animals that can live both in water and on land.

Examples include frogs, toads, and salamanders.

Most amphibians begin life in water and later change as they grow.

Amphibians can live both in water and on land.

Most amphibians start life as eggs in water.

Young amphibians usually breathe with gills, then grow lungs as adults.

Amphibians have smooth, wet skin.
